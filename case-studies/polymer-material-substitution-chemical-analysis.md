# Polymer Material Substitution Chemical Analysis Case Study

This release adds a technical case study on how chemical analysis can identify hidden formulation differences during polymer material substitution.

## Case Summary

In this case, an industrial polymer component originally used an imported material. A domestic substitute material was introduced with similar conventional physical properties, including approximately Shore A 85 hardness. However, the final product showed inconsistent mechanical stability and elastic recovery during practical use.

Xinbodi Laboratories used comparative chemical analysis, GC-MS testing, FTIR spectral fingerprinting, and composition difference evaluation to investigate the root cause.

## Key Findings

The analysis showed that the original and substituted materials were not chemically equivalent, even though their physical properties appeared similar.

Important findings included:

- Differences in additive composition
- Variations in curing-related components
- Differences affecting crosslinking behavior
- Spectral deviations in polymer fingerprint regions
- Formulation-level mismatch behind performance inconsistency

## Result

Based on the analytical findings, the substitute material formulation was adjusted by aligning key additive components with the reference material profile.

After optimization, the material performance improved significantly, and comparative validation indicated approximately 94% similarity in functional performance behavior.
